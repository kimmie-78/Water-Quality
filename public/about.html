About Us - Understanding Water Quality

Water quality is essential for human health, agriculture, and the environment. Ensuring that the water we drink and use is free of harmful contaminants is crucial for our well-being. In this page, we'll explain the most common contaminants found in water and methods to ensure water is safe to use and drink.

Common Contaminants in Water

1. Aluminium

Aluminium is a naturally occurring element, but high levels can enter water sources from industrial waste. Prolonged exposure to aluminium in drinking water can affect kidney function.

2. Arsenic

Arsenic is a toxic element that can contaminate water through natural deposits or industrial pollution. Long-term exposure to arsenic can cause serious health problems, including cancer.

3. Ammonia

Ammonia can be found in drinking water due to agricultural runoff, industrial waste, or the breakdown of organic matter. It can be toxic in high concentrations, affecting the respiratory system.

4. Barium

Barium is a naturally occurring mineral that can seep into groundwater from drilling activities. High levels of barium can lead to cardiovascular and kidney problems.

How to Fix Water Contamination

Fortunately, several methods can help remove harmful contaminants from water and ensure its safety for drinking and other uses. Below are common treatment techniques for each contaminant:

1. Removing Aluminium

Aluminium can be removed through chemical coagulation, where the contaminant binds to chemicals added to water, forming larger particles that can be filtered out.

2. Treating Arsenic Contamination

Arsenic can be effectively removed using reverse osmosis, ion exchange, or activated alumina. These processes filter out the arsenic particles and make water safe to drink.

3. Eliminating Ammonia

Ammonia can be treated through biological filtration or chlorination. These processes neutralize or break down ammonia into safer compounds.

4. Reducing Barium Levels

Barium can be reduced by ion exchange and reverse osmosis. These methods are highly effective at filtering out harmful barium from drinking water.
