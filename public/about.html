About Us - Understanding Water Quality

Water quality is essential for human health, agriculture, and the environment. Ensuring that the water we drink and use is free of harmful contaminants is crucial for our well-being. In this page, we'll explain the most common contaminants found in water and methods to ensure water is safe to use and drink.

Common Contaminants in Water

1. Aluminium

Description: Aluminium is a naturally occurring element, but high levels can enter water sources from industrial waste.

Importance: Prolonged exposure to aluminium in drinking water can affect kidney function.

Fix: Aluminium can be removed through chemical coagulation, where the contaminant binds to chemicals added to water, forming larger particles that can be filtered out.

2. Ammonia

Description: Ammonia can be found in drinking water due to agricultural runoff, industrial waste, or the breakdown of organic matter.

Importance: It can be toxic in high concentrations, affecting the respiratory system.

Fix: Ammonia can be treated through biological filtration or chlorination to neutralize or break it down into safer compounds.

3. Arsenic

Description: Arsenic is a toxic element that can contaminate water through natural deposits or industrial pollution.

Importance: Long-term exposure to arsenic can cause serious health problems, including cancer.

Fix: Arsenic can be effectively removed using reverse osmosis, ion exchange, or activated alumina.

4. Barium

Description: Barium is a naturally occurring mineral that can seep into groundwater from drilling activities.

Importance: High levels of barium can lead to cardiovascular and kidney problems.

Fix: Barium can be reduced by ion exchange and reverse osmosis, which are effective at filtering it from drinking water.

5. Cadmium

Description: Cadmium is a heavy metal that can enter water through industrial discharges and corrosion of pipes.

Importance: Exposure to cadmium can lead to kidney damage and bone loss.

Fix: Cadmium can be removed through reverse osmosis and ion exchange methods.

6. Chloramine

Description: Chloramine is used as a disinfectant in water treatment but can form harmful byproducts.

Importance: High levels can cause respiratory issues and skin irritation.

Fix: Chloramine can be removed by using activated carbon filtration.

7. Chromium

Description: Chromium can enter water from industrial processes and is a known carcinogen.

Importance: Exposure can lead to serious health issues, including cancer.

Fix: Chromium can be treated with reduction methods or through reverse osmosis.

8. Copper

Description: Copper can leach into water from plumbing systems and is a common contaminant.

Importance: Excessive copper can cause gastrointestinal issues.

Fix: Copper levels can be reduced using reverse osmosis and activated carbon filtration.

9. Fluoride

Description: Fluoride is often added to drinking water for dental health but can be harmful in excess.

Importance: High levels can lead to dental and skeletal fluorosis.

Fix: Fluoride can be removed using reverse osmosis systems.

10. Bacteria

Description: Bacteria can contaminate water from sewage and agricultural runoff.

Importance: Pathogenic bacteria can cause serious illness and infections.

Fix: Water can be treated using UV light or chlorination to kill bacteria.

11. Viruses

Description: Viruses can enter water supplies through human or animal waste.

Importance: They can cause waterborne diseases, posing serious health risks.

Fix: Effective filtration and UV treatment can eliminate viruses from water.

12. Lead

Description: Lead can leach into drinking water from old pipes and plumbing fixtures.

Importance: Lead exposure can have serious health impacts, especially in children.

Fix: Lead can be reduced through point-of-use filters or by replacing lead-containing pipes.

13. Nitrates

Description: Nitrates often enter water from fertilizers and sewage.

Importance: High levels can cause methemoglobinemia, or "blue baby syndrome," in infants.

Fix: Nitrates can be removed using ion exchange or reverse osmosis.

14. Nitrites

Description: Nitrites can form from the breakdown of nitrates and are harmful in high concentrations.

Importance: They can affect oxygen transport in the blood.

Fix: Similar to nitrates, nitrites can be removed through ion exchange methods.

15. Mercury

Description: Mercury can enter water through industrial discharges and mining activities.

Importance: It can accumulate in fish and pose serious health risks to humans.

Fix: Mercury can be treated using activated carbon filtration and distillation methods.

16. Perchlorate

Description: Perchlorate is a chemical used in rocket fuel and can contaminate water supplies.

Importance: It can interfere with thyroid function.

Fix: Perchlorate can be removed through ion exchange and reverse osmosis.

17. Radium

Description: Radium is a naturally occurring radioactive element that can contaminate water sources.

Importance: Long-term exposure can increase the risk of cancer.

Fix: Radium can be removed using reverse osmosis and ion exchange methods.

18. Selenium

Description: Selenium can enter water from industrial processes and natural deposits.

Importance: High levels can lead to reproductive and developmental problems.

Fix: Selenium can be treated using biological treatment and filtration methods.

19. Silver

Description: Silver can enter water through industrial waste and can have antimicrobial properties.

Importance: While low levels are not harmful, high concentrations can be toxic.

Fix: Silver can be removed using activated carbon filtration.

20. Uranium

Description: Uranium can contaminate water from natural deposits and mining activities.

Importance: Prolonged exposure can lead to kidney damage and increased cancer risk.

Fix: Uranium can be removed through reverse osmosis and ion exchange methods.
